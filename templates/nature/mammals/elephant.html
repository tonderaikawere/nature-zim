African Elephant

Loxodonta africana

The African elephant is the largest land mammal on Earth. Zimbabwe has one of Africa's largest elephant populations, with over 80,000 elephants roaming the country's national parks and conservancies.

Physical Characteristics

  • Weight: 4,000-7,000 kg
  • Height: 3-4 meters at shoulder
  • Lifespan: 60-70 years
  • Diet: Herbivore (300kg vegetation daily)
  • Gestation: 22 months

Behavior & Ecology

Elephants are highly intelligent and social animals living in matriarchal herds. They play a crucial role as ecosystem engineers, creating water holes and dispersing seeds across vast distances.

Conservation Status

Status: Endangered

Population: ~80,000 in Zimbabwe

Threats: Poaching, habitat loss

Protection: CITES Appendix I
