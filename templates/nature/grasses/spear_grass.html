Spear Grass

Heteropogon contortus

Spear grass is easily recognizable by its distinctive twisted awns that give it its name. This hardy perennial grass is well-adapted to poor soils and dry conditions, making it an important species in Zimbabwe's drier regions.

Distinctive Features

  • Awns: Long, twisted, spear-like structures
  • Height: 30-90 cm
  • Adaptation: Drought-tolerant
  • Soil preference: Poor, rocky soils
  • Grazing value: Moderate palatability
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Heteropogon contortus

Family: Poaceae

Common Names: Spear grass, Tangled spear grass

Distribution: Widespread in Zimbabwe
