African Catfish

Clarias gariepinus

The African Catfish is a hardy and adaptable freshwater fish found throughout Zimbabwe's water bodies. Known for their distinctive barbels (whiskers) and ability to survive in challenging conditions, they are important both ecologically and as a food source.

Adaptations

  • Barbels: Sensory organs for finding food in murky water
  • Air breathing: Can survive in low-oxygen conditions
  • Size: Can grow up to 1.5 meters and 60 kg
  • Diet: Opportunistic omnivore
  • Habitat: Rivers, dams, seasonal pools
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Clarias gariepinus

Family: Clariidae

Lifespan: 8-20 years

Aquaculture: Increasingly farmed
