Zambezi River

Africa's fourth-longest river and Zimbabwe's lifeline

The Zambezi River is one of Africa's great rivers, flowing 2,574 kilometers from its source in Zambia to the Indian Ocean in Mozambique. It forms Zimbabwe's entire northern border and is famous for Victoria Falls, locally known as "Mosi-oa-Tunya" (The Smoke That Thunders).

Key Features

  • Victoria Falls: 108 meters high, 1,708 meters wide
  • Kariba Dam: Creates Lake Kariba, world's largest artificial lake by volume
  • Wildlife: Supports hippos, crocodiles, and diverse fish species
  • Tourism: White-water rafting, fishing, scenic flights
  • Hydropower: Major source of electricity for the region
Quick Facts

Length: 2,574 km

Basin area: 1.33 million km²

Countries: 8 countries in basin

UNESCO Status: Victoria Falls World Heritage Site
