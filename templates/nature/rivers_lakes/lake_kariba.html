Lake Kariba

The world's largest artificial lake by volume

Lake Kariba was created in 1958-1963 by the construction of the Kariba Dam across the Zambezi River. This massive lake is shared between Zimbabwe and Zambia and has become one of the region's most important water resources and tourist destinations.

Significance

  • Size: 5,580 km² surface area, 185 km³ volume
  • Power generation: Major hydroelectric facility
  • Fishing industry: Supports commercial and subsistence fishing
  • Tourism: Houseboats, fishing, wildlife viewing
  • Wildlife: Elephants, hippos, crocodiles, diverse bird life
Quick Facts

Created: 1958-1963

Maximum depth: 97 meters

Shoreline: 2,150 km

Islands: Many, including Chete Island
