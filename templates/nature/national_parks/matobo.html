Matobo Hills National Park

Ancient granite formations and cultural heritage site

The Matobo Hills National Park is famous for its spectacular granite rock formations, ancient San rock art, and cultural significance. This UNESCO World Heritage Site combines natural beauty with rich human history spanning thousands of years.

Highlights

  • Rock formations: Spectacular granite balancing rocks
  • Rock art: Ancient San paintings, some over 2,000 years old
  • Wildlife: Black and white rhinos, leopards, eagles
  • Cultural sites: Cecil Rhodes' grave, sacred sites
  • Birdlife: Highest concentration of black eagles in the world
Quick Facts

Established: 1926

Size: 424 km²

UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site (2003)

Rock art sites: Over 3,000 sites
