Hwange National Park

Zimbabwe's largest national park and elephant sanctuary

Hwange National Park is Zimbabwe's largest national park, covering 14,651 square kilometers. Famous for its massive elephant herds and diverse wildlife, it's one of Africa's premier safari destinations and a crucial conservation area.

Wildlife Highlights

  • Elephants: Over 40,000 elephants, largest population in Zimbabwe
  • Big Five: Lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, rhino
  • Predators: Wild dogs, cheetahs, hyenas
  • Herbivores: Zebra, giraffe, kudu, sable antelope
  • Birds: Over 400 species recorded
Quick Facts

Established: 1928

Size: 14,651 km²

Location: Northwestern Zimbabwe

Best time to visit: May to October
