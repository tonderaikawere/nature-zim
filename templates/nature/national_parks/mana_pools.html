Mana Pools National Park

UNESCO World Heritage Site on the Zambezi River

Mana Pools National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its pristine wilderness and exceptional wildlife viewing opportunities. Located on the banks of the Zambezi River, it offers some of Africa's most authentic safari experiences.

Unique Features

  • Walking safaris: One of few parks allowing guided walks
  • Zambezi River: Spectacular river frontage with islands
  • Seasonal pools: "Mana" means "four" in Shona
  • Canoe safaris: Unique water-based game viewing
  • Pristine wilderness: Minimal human impact
Quick Facts

Established: 1963

Size: 2,196 km²

UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site (1984)

Best time: April to November
