Trees of Zimbabwe

Zimbabwe is home to over 600 tree species, from the iconic Baobab to the economically important hardwoods. Our trees provide shelter, food, medicine, and are integral to our cultural heritage.

Baobab Tree

Scientific Name: Adansonia digitata

The iconic "Tree of Life" can live for over 1,000 years. Its massive trunk stores water during dry seasons, and every part of the tree is useful to humans and animals.

  • Height: Up to 25 meters
  • Habitat: Dry savannas
  • Uses: Food, medicine, shelter
Learn More
Msasa Tree

Scientific Name: Brachystegia spiciformis

Dominant in Zimbabwe's miombo woodlands, the Msasa is crucial for timber production and provides habitat for numerous wildlife species.

  • Height: Up to 20 meters
  • Habitat: Miombo woodlands
  • Uses: Timber, fuel, construction
Learn More
Mopane Tree

Scientific Name: Colophospermum mopane

Adapted to hot, dry conditions, the Mopane forms extensive woodlands in the Lowveld. Its butterfly-shaped leaves are distinctive.

  • Height: 4-18 meters
  • Habitat: Hot, dry lowlands
  • Uses: Timber, fuel, fodder

Did You Know?

  • Zimbabwe has over 600 tree species
  • Miombo woodlands cover 60% of the country
  • Some Baobab trees are over 2,000 years old
  • Trees provide homes for 80% of wildlife
  • One tree produces oxygen for 2 people per day
  • Trees prevent soil erosion and flooding
