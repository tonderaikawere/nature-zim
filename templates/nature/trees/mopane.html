Mopane Tree

Colophospermum mopane

The Mopane tree is one of Zimbabwe's most distinctive trees, easily recognized by its butterfly-shaped leaves. It forms extensive woodlands in the drier regions and is crucial for both wildlife and local communities.

Distinctive Features

  • Leaves: Butterfly-shaped, folding during hot weather
  • Height: 4-18 meters
  • Bark: Grey to reddish-brown, deeply fissured
  • Habitat: Hot, dry areas with clay soils
  • Distribution: Northern and western Zimbabwe
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Colophospermum mopane

Family: Fabaceae

Uses: Construction, fuel, traditional medicine

Wildlife Value: Food for elephants and other herbivores
