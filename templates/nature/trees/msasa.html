Msasa Tree

Brachystegia spiciformis

The Msasa tree is a dominant species in Zimbabwe's miombo woodlands, covering vast areas of the country. This valuable hardwood tree is crucial for both ecological balance and economic purposes, providing timber, fuel, and habitat for numerous species.

Characteristics & Uses

  • Height: Up to 20 meters
  • Habitat: Miombo woodlands, well-drained soils
  • Timber: High-quality hardwood for construction
  • Ecology: Supports diverse wildlife communities
  • Traditional uses: Medicine, tools, building materials
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Brachystegia spiciformis

Family: Fabaceae

Distribution: Miombo woodlands

Conservation: Sustainable harvesting needed
