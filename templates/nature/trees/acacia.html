Acacia Tree

Acacia species

Acacia trees are among the most recognizable trees in Zimbabwe's landscape. With their distinctive umbrella-shaped canopies and thorny branches, they are perfectly adapted to the African savanna and provide crucial resources for wildlife and humans alike.

Characteristics

  • Shape: Distinctive flat-topped, umbrella-like canopy
  • Thorns: Sharp spines for protection from browsers
  • Leaves: Small, compound leaves that reduce water loss
  • Flowers: Yellow, ball-shaped flower clusters
  • Pods: Long, flat seed pods
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Acacia species

Family: Fabaceae

Common Species: Umbrella Thorn, Sweet Thorn

Uses: Shade, gum arabic, traditional medicine
