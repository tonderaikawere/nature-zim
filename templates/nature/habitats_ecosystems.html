Habitats & Ecosystems of Zimbabwe

Explore Zimbabwe's diverse ecosystems and the wildlife they support

Miombo Woodlands

Dominant ecosystem covering 60% of Zimbabwe. Characterized by Brachystegia and Julbernardia trees.

  • Supports diverse wildlife
  • Important for timber and fuel
  • Rich in medicinal plants
Grasslands

Open grasslands support large herbivore populations and are crucial for livestock grazing.

  • High grass diversity
  • Supports grazing animals
  • Fire-adapted ecosystem
Wetlands

Rivers, lakes, and seasonal wetlands provide critical habitat for aquatic species and migratory birds.

  • High biodiversity
  • Water filtration services
  • Flood control

Ecosystem Services

Provisioning Services
  • Food production (crops, livestock, fish)
  • Fresh water supply
  • Timber and fuel wood
  • Medicinal plants
Regulating Services
  • Climate regulation
  • Water purification
  • Soil formation and retention
  • Pollination services

Conservation Priorities

Threats
  • Habitat fragmentation
  • Overgrazing and cultivation
  • Invasive species
  • Climate change
Conservation Actions
  • Protected area management
  • Community conservation programs
  • Restoration projects
  • Sustainable land use planning
