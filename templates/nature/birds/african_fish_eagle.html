African Fish Eagle

Haliaeetus vocifer - Zimbabwe's National Bird

The African Fish Eagle is Zimbabwe's national bird, symbolizing pride and the natural heritage of the country. This magnificent raptor is found near rivers, lakes, and dams throughout Zimbabwe, where its distinctive call echoes across the water.

Physical Features

  • Wingspan: 2-2.4 meters
  • Weight: 2-3.6 kg
  • Length: 63-75 cm
  • Plumage: White head, tail, and chest; black wings and body
  • Call: Distinctive high-pitched cry

Habitat & Behavior

African Fish Eagles are found near water bodies where they hunt for fish, their primary food source. They are skilled fishers, swooping down to snatch fish from the water's surface with their powerful talons.

National Symbol

Status: National Bird

Symbolism: Pride, strength, freedom

Flag: Featured on Zimbabwe's flag

Conservation: Stable population
