Hornbill

Tockus species

Hornbills are distinctive birds with large, curved bills and unique nesting behaviors. Several species are found in Zimbabwe, playing important roles as seed dispersers in forest ecosystems.

Characteristics

  • Bill: Large, curved, often with casque on top
  • Size: Varies by species, 30-60 cm
  • Nesting: Unique sealed nest behavior
  • Diet: Fruits, insects, small animals
  • Habitat: Woodlands and forests
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Tockus species

Family: Bucerotidae

Common Species: Red-billed, Yellow-billed

Role: Important seed dispersers
