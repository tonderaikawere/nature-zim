Weaver Bird

Ploceus species

Weaver birds are master architects of the bird world, famous for their intricate woven nests. These social birds are common throughout Zimbabwe and are known for their bright yellow plumage and complex colonial nesting behavior.

Characteristics

  • Nests: Intricately woven hanging structures
  • Breeding plumage: Bright yellow and black (males)
  • Behavior: Highly social, colonial nesting
  • Diet: Seeds, insects, nectar
  • Habitat: Woodlands, gardens, near water
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Ploceus species

Family: Ploceidae

Common Species: Village Weaver, Masked Weaver

Nest Material: Grass strips, palm fronds
