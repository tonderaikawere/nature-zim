Nile Crocodile

Crocodylus niloticus

The Nile Crocodile is Africa's largest freshwater predator and one of Zimbabwe's most formidable reptiles. Found in the Zambezi River, Lake Kariba, and other water bodies, these ancient predators have remained virtually unchanged for millions of years.

Characteristics

  • Size: Males up to 5 meters, females up to 3.5 meters
  • Weight: Large males can exceed 500 kg
  • Bite force: One of the strongest in the animal kingdom
  • Lifespan: 70-100 years
  • Habitat: Rivers, lakes, wetlands
Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Crocodylus niloticus

Family: Crocodylidae

Status: Least Concern

Diet: Fish, birds, mammals

Nesting: Builds nests on riverbanks
