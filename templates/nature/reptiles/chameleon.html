Chameleon

Various species

Chameleons are among Zimbabwe's most fascinating reptiles, famous for their ability to change color and their unique adaptations. These arboreal lizards have independently moving eyes, projectile tongues, and specialized feet for gripping branches.

Unique Adaptations

  • Color change: For communication and temperature regulation
  • Eyes: Can move independently in different directions
  • Tongue: Projectile tongue longer than body
  • Feet: Specialized for gripping branches
  • Tail: Prehensile, acts as fifth limb
Quick Facts

Family: Chamaeleonidae

Species in Zimbabwe: 10+ species

Size range: 5-30 cm

Diet: Insects and small invertebrates

Habitat: Trees and shrubs
