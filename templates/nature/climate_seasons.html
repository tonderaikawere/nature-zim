Climate & Seasons of Zimbabwe

Understanding Zimbabwe's climate patterns and seasonal changes

Wet Season

Period: November to March

Hot and humid with afternoon thunderstorms. This is the growing season when most plants flower and animals give birth.

Cool Dry Season

Period: April to August

Pleasant temperatures with clear skies. Best time for wildlife viewing as animals congregate around water sources.

Hot Dry Season

Period: September to October

Hot and dry conditions before the rains. Wildlife concentrates around permanent water sources.

Climate Zones

Highveld (above 1,200m)
  • Cooler temperatures year-round
  • Higher rainfall (750-1,000mm annually)
  • Includes Harare and surrounding areas
Lowveld (below 900m)
  • Hotter temperatures
  • Lower rainfall (400-650mm annually)
  • Includes Zambezi and Limpopo valleys

Climate Change Impacts

Observed Changes
  • Increasing temperatures
  • More erratic rainfall patterns
  • More frequent droughts
  • Shifting growing seasons
Adaptation Strategies
  • Drought-resistant crop varieties
  • Water conservation techniques
  • Early warning systems
  • Ecosystem-based adaptation
