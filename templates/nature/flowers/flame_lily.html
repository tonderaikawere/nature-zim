Flame Lily - Zimbabwe's National Flower

Gloriosa superba

The Flame Lily is Zimbabwe's national flower, chosen for its striking beauty and symbolic significance. Its distinctive red and yellow petals curve backward like flames, giving it its common name. This climbing plant is found throughout Zimbabwe and holds special cultural importance.

Characteristics

  • Petals: Bright red and yellow, curved backward
  • Growth habit: Climbing vine up to 4 meters
  • Flowering time: October to March (rainy season)
  • Habitat: Woodland margins, grasslands
  • Propagation: Underground tubers

Cultural Significance

The Flame Lily was chosen as Zimbabwe's national flower because it represents the country's beauty, resilience, and vibrant spirit. It appears on Zimbabwe's coat of arms and is featured in various cultural celebrations and ceremonies.

Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Gloriosa superba

Family: Colchicaceae

Status: National Flower

Conservation: Protected species

Uses: Ornamental, cultural ceremonies
